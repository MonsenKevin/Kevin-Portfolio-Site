Ohlone Lodge: Cloud Migration & Compliance

Google Workspace DNS / MX Records Data Migration Service IT Administration

Orchestrated a critical infrastructure upgrade for the Ohlone Lodge (Pacific Skyline Council), transitioning 50+ users from a legacy Network Solutions environment to Google Workspace for Nonprofits. The primary objective was to eliminate recurring hosting costs while establishing a modern, secure communication stack.

The technical execution involved navigating complex validation protocols with Google Support and TechSoup to secure non-profit eligibility. I utilized Google's Data Migration Service (DMS) to transfer historical data, mapping legacy addresses to personal accounts. By lowering DNS TTL settings prior to the switch, I achieved a zero-downtime transition during the cutover.

A key innovation of this project was engineering a solution for "Two-Deep Leadership" safety compliance. I configured the email architecture to function as a routing layer; emails sent to youth leadership positions are now automatically forwarded to both the youth member and a designated adult advisor.

This automated routing ensures strict adherence to youth protection policies without relying on manual cc'ing. The project resulted in 100% savings on annual email fees ($200/yr), improved deliverability via authenticated DKIM/SPF records, and a safer digital environment for the organization.
